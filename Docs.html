Research, Memos & Analysis

This section contains written material explaining why Digital Action Receipts exist, the problems they address, and the contexts in which durable, customer-custodied evidence becomes necessary.

What lives here

  • Conceptual memos on digital accountability
  • Analyses of audit failures, disputes, and investigations
  • Interpretations of regulatory and legal expectations
  • Post-incident reasoning (what evidence was missing)
  • Foundational thinking behind DAR’s design boundaries

What does not live here

  • API documentation or SDKs
  • Implementation details or schemas
  • Infrastructure configurations
  • Step-by-step integration guides

Technical enablement belongs in private or gated documentation.

Why this matters

Many accountability failures are not technical—they are evidentiary. Systems function, but proof does not survive disputes, audits, or time. These documents focus on that gap.

Publishing approach

Content is published intentionally and sparingly. Each document is meant to stand on its own as a durable explanation, not as marketing or instruction.

  • Quality over volume
  • Primary sources where possible
  • Neutral, non-promotional tone
  • Focused on evidence, not opinion
