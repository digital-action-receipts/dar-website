Customer-owned evidence

A receipt is the authority — not the vendor database.

Digital Action Receipts (DAR) are signed, tamper-evident evidence artifacts emitted after digital actions commit. They are stored in customer-controlled immutable storage and can be independently verified later for audits, disputes, investigations, and AI accountability.

What DAR is

  • Evidence of what happened, when it happened, and who/what performed it
  • Issued at action time (not reconstructed later)
  • Customer-custodied (written to customer-controlled storage)
  • Independently verifiable (integrity can be checked outside the issuing system)
  • Minimal by design (stores references to context, not sensitive payloads)

DAR is not monitoring. DAR is not decision logic. DAR is not a system of record.

Where the receipts live

Receipts are written into customer-controlled immutable storage. This preserves custody and avoids a critical dispute failure mode: the vendor controlling the authoritative evidence.

  • AWS S3 Object Lock
  • Azure Immutable Blob
  • GCP Retention Bucket

DAR may provide issuance and verification tooling, but does not become the owner of your evidence.

Why now

Systems are increasingly automated and agent-driven. When something goes wrong, the demand is the same: “Prove what happened under which conditions.” DAR makes that proof portable.

What a receipt preserves

A receipt anchors the action and the referenced context that matters for accountability: policy version references, model version hashes, configuration references, and stable correlation.

What DAR avoids

DAR avoids becoming surveillance or a data lake. The goal is defensible evidence with minimal retained data and clear custody boundaries.

How to think about it

If a vendor system disappears, changes, or is disputed, the receipt remains valid. Like a paper receipt: the database is a convenience; the receipt is the authority.

  • Action happens → an evidence artifact is emitted after the action commits
  • Evidence is stored → in customer-controlled immutable storage
  • Later verification → receipts can be checked for integrity and reviewed as a timeline
