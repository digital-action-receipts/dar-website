Neutral infrastructure primitive

Verifiable receipts for digital actions.

Digital Action Receipts (DARs) are issued at the moment an action occurs and preserved as append-only, tamper-evident records. They provide a durable reference to what happened—before disputes, audits, incident reviews, or narrative reconstruction.

DAR = a verifiable, append-only record of a digital action, issued at execution time. It preserves: actor · action · object · timestamp · reference · policy/context (optional) · integrity proof

What DAR is

A small, durable artifact that proves an action occurred. DAR records facts, not interpretations.

append-only tamper-evident verifiable

What DAR is not

Not monitoring. Not dashboards. Not policy enforcement. Not a compliance product. DAR does not decide correctness; it preserves evidence.

no alerts no scoring no interpretation

Why it exists

When systems are questioned later, mutable logs and narratives fail. DAR provides a stable reference that survives remediation, vendor changes, and hindsight.

disputes audits incidents

Integration posture

DAR is designed for low-friction adoption. Integrations should take minutes, not quarters. The contract is intentionally small: issue, verify, query by reference.

await issueReceipt({ actor: "system|user|agent", action: "approved", object: "loan_application", ref: "internal-id-123", policy: "policy-version-or-rule-id" // optional })

See Integration for the canonical SDK contract, receipt schema, and storage/verification model.

Scope, boundaries, and authority

DAR focuses on durable evidence at the action boundary. It does not interpret events, diagnose intent, or replace existing systems. The goal is to preserve what occurred—so audits, disputes, and investigations can be anchored to facts rather than reconstructed narratives.

For explicit non-goals and responsibility boundaries, see Principles.
