System Boundaries & Non-Goals

Digital Action Receipts are intentionally narrow. This page states, explicitly, what the system does not do. These boundaries protect correctness, prevent scope creep, and reduce misinterpretation.

Back to Definition Docs index

Boundary statement

Digital Action Receipts record occurrence, not outcomes. They generate a verifiable, append-only record at the Moment of Action, and support Verification of integrity only. They do not explain, judge, validate, enforce, or optimize decisions.
  • The system’s purpose is to create a referenceable record that an action occurred at a particular time.
  • Verification confirms whether a receipt has been altered or mismatched.
  • Anything involving correctness, justification, or enforcement is out of scope.

Non-goals (explicit exclusions)

Not a logging system
Digital Action Receipts do not replace operational logs or application logging. They are not intended for debugging, observability, or performance tracing.
Not an audit trail
Digital Action Receipts are not designed as a complete historical narrative of system behavior. They do not guarantee completeness, coverage, or policy alignment.
Not a compliance tool
Digital Action Receipts do not determine compliance, produce compliance conclusions, or certify adherence to regulations or internal controls.
Not decision validation
Verification is not correctness validation. A verified receipt only indicates integrity of the recorded receipt, not that the action was correct or permitted.
Not explainability
Digital Action Receipts do not generate explanations, rationales, summaries, or narratives about why an action occurred or what the actor intended.
Not enforcement
Digital Action Receipts do not block actions, approve actions, deny actions, or enforce rules. They are not a gatekeeper or policy engine.
Not monitoring or analytics
Digital Action Receipts do not provide dashboards, trend monitoring, anomaly detection, or behavioral scoring. Any analytics overlays are out of scope for this primitive.
Not reconstruction
The system is not designed to reconstruct internal state or infer what “really happened” beyond the receipt. The receipt is generated at the Moment of Action, not reconstructed after the fact.

Integrity-only verification

Verification confirms integrity only. No correctness • No intent • No enforcement.
  • Verification determines whether a receipt appears altered or mismatched relative to its Integrity Information.
  • Verification does not validate that the action should have happened, was justified, or produced a correct outcome.
  • Verification does not authenticate identity beyond what the Action-Originating System represents.

Coexistence with existing records

  • Digital Action Receipts coexist with logs, metrics, and audit processes.
  • Operational records may provide context; receipts provide a stable reference point for “this occurred.”
  • The system does not require replacing existing tooling or process.

Scope control (what would be separate invention families)

Extensions that introduce event detection, monitoring, policy evaluation, or automated interpretation are intentionally separate. If included at all, they should be treated as separate invention families and separate product surfaces.
  • Event-triggering systems that decide when to trigger a receipt
  • Boundary/rule receipts (BRR) that formalize rule boundaries or governance events
  • Analytics overlays, scoring, monitoring, or anomaly detection
  • Decision explanation layers or narrative generation
